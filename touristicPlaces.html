Tourist and Historical places in Egypt

Alexandria

  • New Alexandria Library :

    The Bibliotheca Alexandrina is one of the newest and most prominent cultural monuments in Egypt and the world and contains more than 8 million books

  • Citadel of Qaitbay :

    The Qaitbay Citadel is located in the Bahri area in the far west of Alexandria. It was built on the ancient site of the Lighthouse of Alexandria, Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay. The construction of this castle

  • Stanley Bridge :

    Stanley Bridge is the first bridge in Egypt to be constructed over the sea, he bridge preserves the architectural style of the famous and cosmopolitan Egyptian city


Aswan

  • Abu Simbel :

    An archaeological site located on the western bank of Lake Nasser. Aswan. It is one of the sites of the "Nubian Monuments" included in the UNESCO List of World Heritage Sites

  • Nubia Museum :

    It displays more than 5,000 artifacts from the antiquities of ancient Nubia. The architecture of the museum was designed by the Egyptian engineer Mahmoud al-Hakim. The design came in harmony with the surrounding environment. It has rocks and hills and the nature of the bright and hot sun of the city of Aswan.

  • Plant Island :

    The Botanical Garden is located in Aswan on an entire island.

The_Pyramids

  • Pyramid of Khufu :

    It was built by King Khufu. the only surviving miracle of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The original height of the Great Pyramid is 146 meters, and currently it is approximately 137 meters, and the length of its base side is 230 meters

  • Pyramid of Khafre :

    It was built by King Khafre, the fourth king of the Fourth Dynasty, the son of King Khufu. Its height was 143 meters and now it is 136 meters. It was built over an area of ​​215 square meters.

  • Menkaure's pyramid :

    is the smallest of the three pyramids of Giza in Egypt . built by King Menkaure, son of King Khafre of the Fourth Egyptian Dynasty . The length of each of its sides is 108.5 meters, its height is 65.5 meters, and it is now 62 meters long after the fall of its outer covering


The_Red_Sea

  • The short :

    the city is a quiet tourist resort with sandy beaches, clear water and coral reefs, in addition to a group of Christian and Islamic monuments dating back to different eras, for example, there is a castle built in 1799 AD and the mausoleum of Sheikh Abu al-Hasan al-Shazly, which was established in 1892, in addition to some Coptic, Roman and Islamic monuments. Pharaonic antiquities dating back to the era of Queen Hatshepsut and a gold mine in Wadi Al-Fawakhir.

  • Wadi El-Gemal and Hamata Mountain Reserve :

    It is located in the south of the Red Sea Governorate, with a total area of ​​7,450 km. The reserve consists of multiple environmental elements, including the Wadi El-Gamal Basin, which is one of the largest and richest wadis in the Eastern Desert, Mount Hamata, which is the highest mountain in the Eastern Desert, mangrove forests that extend along the coasts of the region, and coral reefs that still preserve their nature. Pristine sea beaches that contain islands where birds and sea turtles breed, and sandy beaches that are characterized by a large number of sea turtles.
